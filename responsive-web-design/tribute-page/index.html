About

Jose Rizal, born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines, is a legendary figure known for his significant contributions to the fight for Philippine independence and his literary works that exposed the injustices under Spanish colonial rule.

His dedication to the Filipino cause and ultimate sacrifice made him an immortal hero in the hearts of the Filipino people.

Key Achievements:

  • Author of "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," powerful novels that exposed Spanish colonial abuses.
  • Founding member of the La Liga Filipina, an organization advocating for civil rights and reforms.
  • Contributed significantly to Filipino literature, poetry, and essays.
  • Advocated for education and reforms in the Philippines.
  • Executed on December 30, 1896, for his involvement in the Philippine Revolution, further igniting the fight for independence.
